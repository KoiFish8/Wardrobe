# AI Wardrobe Stylist — Marketing Playbook

*Use this AFTER the app exists. Marketing = customer acquisition = the single riskiest assumption in the business. Treat content + small-creator seeding as the experiment that de-risks the whole venture.*

---

## 0. The core truth

In this niche, **simple beats polished**, and the product is **faceless-native** — the hero content is clothes and the app screen, never your face. The hard part is never the editing. It's the hook (first 1–2 seconds) and posting consistently through the early flops. Don't let tool research become procrastination.

---

## 1. The minimal content stack (all free)

- **CapCut** — ~90% of all editing: auto-captions, text overlays, trimming, trending-audio library. Built for beginners.
- **Phone screen recorder** — captures the app in action. Already on your phone.
- **Canva** (optional) — text graphics / thumbnails only.

That's the whole stack. Don't add to it.

## 2. Three faceless formats (no face, ever)

1. **Text-on-screen + trending audio, no voice.** Lowest effort, most common. Screen-record or film clothes, add a trending sound, tell the story in short captions.
2. **Hands-and-clothes only.** Laying out outfits, folding, holding pieces up — hands and garments in frame.
3. **AI voiceover (optional).** ElevenLabs for natural narration from typed text. Skip early — native trending audio performs better with the algorithm.

## 3. First-video workflow (~30 min)

> Screen-record the app generating outfits from your real closet → export → CapCut → hook caption in the first second → trending sound → auto-caption → post.

## 4. Hook templates (first 1–2 seconds is everything)

- "Every outfit from a 15-piece wardrobe."
- "POV: you own nothing but have nothing to wear — watch this."
- "I scanned my entire closet and the app found [X] outfits I never saw."
- "The one $30 buy that unlocked 9 new outfits."
- "How to never repeat an outfit with 12 pieces."
- "Rating my own closet so you don't have to think."

## 5. Content formats that fit this niche

- **Closet-in-a-video:** every outfit from a small wardrobe.
- **Gap-analysis reveal:** "the single piece that unlocked the most outfits" — this demos your differentiator and is inherently shareable.
- **Before/after:** messy/overwhelming closet → organized + outfit-ready.
- **Capsule challenge:** "1 week, 10 pieces, 7 outfits."
- **Relatable problem:** "me staring at a full closet feeling like I own nothing."

## 6. Starter batch — 10 videos you can film from your own closet

1. Every outfit from your real wardrobe (the flagship).
2. Gap analysis: the one buy that unlocks the most outfits.
3. "I'm not a fashion person, here's how I get dressed now."
4. Scanning my whole closet in 60 seconds (the app demo).
5. 5 outfits, all neutrals, one small wardrobe (your actual closet is all neutrals — lean in).
6. "Rate my closet" / closet tour, faceless.
7. Packing a carry-on using only outfits the app generated.
8. Same 3 pieces styled 5 ways.
9. "What to buy next" — let the app pick, react to it.
10. Common mistake: why you feel like you have nothing to wear (problem-agitate, then the app as solution).

## 7. Posting cadence

- **Volume early.** Post daily or near-daily for the first 4–6 weeks. The first ~10 will likely flop — that's normal and not a signal to stop.
- **Repurpose one video across TikTok + Reels + Shorts.** Same export, three platforms.
- **Double down on whatever travels.** When one format pops, make five more of that exact thing.
- **Success signal:** you're looking for *one* format that consistently outperforms — that becomes your engine.

---

## 8. Sponsoring smaller creators

Right lever for a small budget. Bigger isn't better here — **nano and micro creators have higher engagement, more authentic pull, and fit the "I'm not a fashion person" lane** better than polished fashion influencers.

### Current rates (2026)

| Tier | Followers | TikTok video | Instagram Reel | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K–10K | ~$25–200 | ~$50–300 | Often open to gifting/affiliate deals |
| Micro | 10K–100K | ~$200–1,500 | higher | More reach, less personal |

Reels cost 2–3x a static post. Usage rights add 20–50%. TikTok is cheaper than Instagram at these tiers.

### Strategy for *this* app

- **Seed many nano creators, not one micro.** A $1,000 budget across ~10 nano creators ($50–150 each) beats one $1,000 micro post — more shots on goal, more authentic, spreads risk.
- **Target the right niches:** minimalism, capsule wardrobe, "outfit ideas," budget/college style, decluttering — *not* high-fashion.
- **The strongest format is the creator using the app on their own closet.** An authentic demo ("I scanned my closet with this app") outperforms a scripted ad read every time. Brief them loosely, let them be themselves.
- **Deal structures, in order of preference for a small budget:**
  1. **Gifted Pro + affiliate/commission + unique promo code.** Performance-aligned, low upfront cost. The code is non-negotiable — it's how you measure.
  2. **Small flat fee ($50–150) for nano creators** who won't do pure performance.
  3. Avoid big flat fees until you've proven a creator/format converts.
- **Always attribute.** Unique promo codes or tracked links per creator. Without attribution you can't tell winners from money pits — and most uninstrumented influencer spend is wasted.

### Honest expectations

- **Treat it as a portfolio, not a bet.** Most sponsored posts will underperform; one or two will carry the whole campaign. That's the normal shape — budget for the misses.
- **Start with a small test batch** (5–10 nano creators, ~$500–1,500 total), measure by code, then double down only on what actually drove installs.
- **Do it after the app is solid.** Driving installs to a buggy app burns both money and the creators' trust.
- **Your own organic content comes first.** If *you* can't make a closet video that travels, paying someone else to won't fix the underlying idea. Prove the format organically, then pay creators to scale it.

---

## 9. The cheapest thing to do first

Before spending a dollar on creators: make 5 of your own faceless closet videos and post them. If any travel, you've validated the riskiest assumption in the business for free — and you've learned the format you'll later pay creators to replicate. If none do, fix the hook/format before scaling spend.
